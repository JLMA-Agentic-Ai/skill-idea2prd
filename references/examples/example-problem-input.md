# Example Problem Inputs for Idea-to-PRD Skill

This document provides sample problem statements that demonstrate how to structure problem descriptions for optimal analysis by the idea-to-prd skill's problem-solving pipeline.

## Problem Input Structure

### Basic Problem Template
```
Problem: [Clear, specific problem statement]

Context: [Background information and current situation]

Impact: [Who is affected and how]

Constraints: [Limitations and requirements]

Goals: [Desired outcomes and success criteria]
```

## Simple Problem Examples

### Customer Service Problem
```
Problem: Customer support tickets take 48+ hours to resolve, leading to customer frustration and churn.

Context: Growing user base but limited support staff. Most issues are repetitive questions that could be automated.

Impact: Customer satisfaction scores dropping, 15% increase in churn rate, support team burnout.

Goals: Reduce response time to under 2 hours, improve customer satisfaction by 40%.
```

### Remote Work Problem
```
Problem: Remote team members feel disconnected and less productive during virtual meetings.

Context: Company went fully remote during pandemic, using basic video conferencing tools without collaboration features.

Impact: Meeting engagement down 30%, project delays increasing, team morale declining.

Goals: Improve meeting engagement, reduce project delivery time, strengthen team connections.
```

## Detailed Problem Examples

### Healthcare Administrative Burden
```
Problem Statement:
Healthcare providers spend 60-70% of their time on administrative tasks rather than direct patient care, contributing to provider burnout and reduced healthcare quality.

Context and Background:
- Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems require extensive manual data entry
- Insurance authorization processes involve repetitive form filling and phone calls
- Patient scheduling requires constant human intervention and coordination
- Quality reporting and compliance documentation consume increasing amounts of time
- Multiple systems don't communicate effectively, requiring duplicate data entry

Current State Analysis:
- Average physician spends 2+ hours on documentation for every hour of patient care
- Nurse practitioners report administrative tasks as primary source of job dissatisfaction
- Insurance authorization delays affect 40% of specialist appointments
- Smaller practices consider closing due to administrative overhead

Affected Stakeholders:
- Healthcare Providers (physicians, nurses, specialists)
- Administrative Staff (billing, scheduling, records)
- Patients (experiencing delays and reduced face-time with providers)
- Healthcare Organizations (facing efficiency and profitability challenges)
- Insurance Companies (processing inefficient authorization requests)

Impact Assessment:
- Provider burnout rates at 50%+ across specialties
- Patient satisfaction scores declining due to rushed visits
- Healthcare costs increasing due to administrative inefficiencies
- Rural healthcare access decreasing as practices close
- Medical errors potentially increasing due to documentation burdens

Constraints and Requirements:
- Must maintain HIPAA compliance and patient data security
- Integration with existing EHR systems required
- Cannot disrupt patient care or safety protocols
- Budget limitations for smaller practices and rural clinics
- Regulatory requirements for documentation cannot be eliminated
- Provider workflow disruptions must be minimized during implementation

Desired Outcomes:
- Reduce administrative time by 50% while maintaining compliance
- Improve provider satisfaction and reduce burnout indicators
- Enhance patient care quality through increased face-to-face time
- Streamline insurance authorization processes
- Enable smaller practices to remain viable and serve communities
```

### E-commerce Cart Abandonment
```
Problem Statement:
E-commerce platforms experience 70% cart abandonment rates, resulting in significant revenue loss and poor customer experience.

Context and Background:
- Online shopping cart abandonment is a persistent industry-wide challenge
- Customers add items to cart but leave without completing purchase
- Traditional recovery methods (email reminders) have low effectiveness
- Mobile commerce abandonment rates even higher (85%+)
- Checkout processes often complex and time-consuming

Current State Analysis:
- Average cart abandonment rate: 69.82% across all industries
- Fashion and luxury goods: 84.3% abandonment rate
- Mobile abandonment rates 20% higher than desktop
- Recovery email open rates: 14-18%
- Multi-step checkout processes correlate with higher abandonment

Root Causes Identified:
- Unexpected shipping costs revealed at checkout
- Complicated or lengthy registration requirements
- Concerns about payment security
- Limited payment method options
- Slow page loading times
- Distracting website elements during checkout
- Lack of trust indicators
- No guest checkout option

Affected Stakeholders:
- E-commerce Businesses (lost revenue and customer insights)
- Customers (frustrating shopping experiences)
- Marketing Teams (wasted acquisition costs)
- Customer Service (handling abandonment-related inquiries)
- Developers (constantly optimizing checkout flows)

Business Impact:
- $18 billion in lost revenue annually in US e-commerce
- Customer acquisition costs increase when purchases don't complete
- Reduced customer lifetime value
- Poor user experience affects brand perception
- Competitive disadvantage against optimized competitors

Constraints and Considerations:
- Must maintain PCI compliance for payment processing
- Cannot compromise security for convenience
- Different customer segments have varying preferences
- Mobile-first design requirements
- International payment method support needed
- Integration with existing e-commerce platforms
- Performance impact must be minimal

Success Criteria:
- Reduce cart abandonment rate by 25% (from 70% to 52.5%)
- Increase conversion rate by 15%
- Improve customer satisfaction scores for checkout experience
- Reduce customer service inquiries related to purchase issues
- Maintain or improve transaction security standards
```

### Remote Learning Engagement
```
Problem Statement:
Students in online learning environments show poor engagement and learning retention, with course completion rates below 15% and minimal skill application.

Detailed Problem Analysis:
Current online education platforms focus primarily on content delivery rather than learning optimization. Students struggle with motivation, accountability, and practical application of knowledge in isolation.

Context and Background:
- COVID-19 accelerated adoption of online learning across all age groups
- Traditional classroom engagement strategies don't translate to virtual environments
- One-size-fits-all approach doesn't accommodate diverse learning styles
- Limited interaction between students and instructors
- Difficulty in assessing real understanding vs. surface-level completion
- Lack of practical, hands-on experience in virtual settings

Quantified Problem Metrics:
- Average online course completion rate: 14.9%
- Student engagement drops 40% after first week
- Knowledge retention after 6 months: 25% vs. 70% for in-person learning
- Time-to-competency 2.5x longer than traditional learning
- Student satisfaction scores 30% lower than classroom equivalents

Stakeholder Impact Analysis:

Students:
- Frustration with self-directed learning requirements
- Isolation and lack of peer interaction
- Difficulty maintaining motivation and discipline
- Poor learning outcomes affecting career prospects

Instructors:
- Limited ability to gauge student understanding
- Reduced interaction and relationship building
- Difficulty adapting teaching methods to virtual environment
- Increased time spent on individual student support

Educational Institutions:
- High dropout rates affecting reputation and revenue
- Increased student support resource requirements
- Pressure to maintain accreditation standards
- Competition from alternative learning platforms

Employers:
- Graduates lacking practical skills despite certification completion
- Need for additional training of employees with online education backgrounds
- Difficulty assessing candidate competencies from online credentials

Underlying Root Causes:
- Passive content consumption vs. active learning engagement
- Lack of immediate feedback and course correction
- Missing social learning and peer collaboration elements
- Inadequate assessment of practical skill application
- Technology barriers and learning curve issues
- Distractions in home/remote learning environments

Constraints and Requirements:
- Must accommodate diverse technical skill levels
- Accessibility requirements for students with disabilities
- Cost-effectiveness compared to traditional education models
- Scalability to serve large student populations
- Integration with existing Learning Management Systems (LMS)
- Compliance with educational standards and accreditation requirements

Desired Transformation:
- Increase course completion rates to 70%+
- Improve learning retention to match or exceed in-person outcomes
- Enable personalized learning paths based on individual progress
- Foster meaningful peer-to-peer interaction and collaboration
- Provide practical, hands-on experience within virtual constraints
- Create accountability mechanisms that support student success
- Deliver measurable skill competencies that employers value
```

## Problem Input Best Practices

### ✅ Good Problem Characteristics

**Specific and Measurable**
```
❌ "Our website is slow"
✅ "Page load times exceed 3 seconds on mobile devices, causing 25% of users to abandon before content loads"
```

**Includes Context and Impact**
```
❌ "Users don't like our app"
✅ "User retention drops 60% after first week due to complex onboarding process, with exit surveys citing 'too confusing' as primary reason"
```

**Identifies Stakeholders**
```
❌ "This affects everyone"
✅ "Primary impact on customer service reps (handling 40% more complaints) and end customers (NPS score dropped from 7.2 to 4.1)"
```

### ❌ Problem Input Anti-Patterns

**Too Vague**
```
"People have problems with technology"
"Business isn't doing well"
"Need better communication"
```

**Solution-Oriented (Not Problem-Focused)**
```
"We need an AI chatbot"
"Build a mobile app"
"Implement microservices"
```

**Lack of Context**
```
"Customers complain"
"Sales are down"
"Team is stressed"
```

## Input Validation and Security

### Sensitive Information to Exclude
- Specific financial data or revenue figures
- Customer names or identifying information
- Proprietary processes or trade secrets
- Internal organizational details
- Competitive intelligence

### Safe Problem Context to Include
- General industry challenges
- Anonymized user behavior patterns
- Public market trends
- Common operational metrics
- Standard technical limitations

## Expected Processing Flow

When you submit a problem input, the skill will:

1. **Problem Analysis Phase** (Using explore skill)
   - Clarify problem scope and context
   - Identify key stakeholders
   - Ask targeted follow-up questions

2. **Research Phase** (Using goap-research skill)
   - Investigate existing solutions
   - Analyze market approaches
   - Identify technology options

3. **Solution Generation** (Using problem-solver-enhanced skill)
   - Apply multiple problem-solving frameworks
   - Generate potential solution approaches
   - Synthesize optimal product direction

4. **PRD Generation**
   - Transform solution into structured product requirements
   - Define implementation roadmap
   - Create comprehensive documentation

Use these examples to structure your problem statements for optimal analysis and solution generation.
